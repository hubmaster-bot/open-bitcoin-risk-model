# Research Handbook — Validation Philosophy

## Evidence before confidence

A model should not earn trust because it looks plausible.

It should earn trust through:

- transparent construction,
- no-look-ahead calculations,
- historical testing,
- walk-forward validation,
- documented limitations,
- and reproducible results.

## What validation can show

Validation can show whether the historical model separated different outcome environments.

## What validation cannot show

Validation cannot prove:

- future profitability,
- future risk reduction,
- strategy suitability,
- or permanent model validity.

## Small sample problem

Bitcoin has experienced only a limited number of major market cycles.

Thousands of daily observations do not equal thousands of independent cycles.

## Ongoing standard

Every new Market Dimension should:

1. answer a distinct question,
2. use transparent inputs,
3. avoid future leakage,
4. improve historical evidence,
5. and justify its complexity.
